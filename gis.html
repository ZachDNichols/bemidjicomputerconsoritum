What is GIS?

A Geographic Information System, or GIS for short, is a computer system that analyzes and displays geographically referenced information. It uses data that is attatched to a unique location. GIS has many implications, especially in earth sciences and biology. It is a computer software that requires no coding experience and bridges the gap between earth sciences and computer science.
Source: USGS.gov

What are its uses?

GIS technology is often used to conduct scientific investigations, manage resources, as well as aid in development planning. According to National Geographic; many retail businesses will use GIS software to help them determine where to locate a new store. Marketing companies will use GIS to decide to whom to market stores and resturaunts , as well as where that marketing should be.
